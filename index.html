As music lovers, we have a picture that we can play the piano professionally someday, and we can earn flashy applause at a party or public concert. The reality is that people who have started to learn the piano need to pay a considerable amount of money to purchase an instrument and find a tutor. They also have to invest massive time in learning fundamental entry-level theories. As time goes by, more and more learners feel tired, hardly see payback from experience, and give up the hobby. In-person teaching is also more and more faded away from the mainstream since the post-covid century coming. At this point, we need to find a way to help music lovers more delightfully enjoy the joy of learning an instrument and get the skill on their hand quickly and sufficiently.
